ASCII Art

We're going to play around a little more with ASCII art during this assignment. We've created 24 text files, formatted as {i}.txt where i ranges from 0 to 23. The files are hosted at the URLs https://stanfordpython.com/row-of-puzzles/ascii/0.txt through https://stanfordpython.com/row-of-puzzles/ascii/23.txt.

Each of the files is formatted in the following way:

                
char, num
char, num
char, num
...
For example, 1.txt looks like: 
                
 , 44
:, 4
 , 26
:, 4
 , 1

Each of these files represents a row in a multiline string. To convert the file to a row, you have to generate num many copies of char, for each of the rows in the text file, and then combine each of those strings, in order. For example, 1.txt represents the string that is 44 spaces, followed by 4 colons, followed by 26 spaces, followed by 4 colons, followed by a space.

Then, the string that will reveal the solution to this problem can be found by printing each of the rows, one after another.
